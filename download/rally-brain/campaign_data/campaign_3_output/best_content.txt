Spent last week digging into how use actually works in DeFi and most of it feels like walking on a tightrope. You get the upside but one bad wick wipes you out completely. Fragments by Ampleforth takes a totally different approach. They use tranching to split cbBTC into two tokens. You have the Senior tranche which is stable and earns yield from the use demand. Then you have Bitcoin Junior. It gets the amplified exposure, about 1.33x, without any debt or borrowing. Since there is no loan structure, you can't get liquidated. It is built for holding through cycles, not for active trading. I think the backtests from 2023 to 2024 showing it outperforming raw BTC are interesting, but I'm curious how it handles a prolonged bear market. You can usually swap back to cbBTC which helps. @RallyOnChain flagged this early. It feels like a cleaner way to get exposure without babysitting margin positions. Check the waitlist at fragments.org and let me know if you think this structure actually replaces traditional use.
