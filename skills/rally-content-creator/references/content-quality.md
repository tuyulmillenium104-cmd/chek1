# Rally.fun Content Quality Framework

## AI Banned Words (ZERO TOLERANCE)

These words immediately signal "AI-generated" to Rally.fun's AI judge. Any detection results in score penalties.

### Full Banned List
```
delve, leverage, paradigm, tapestry, landscape, nuance, crucial, pivotal,
embark, harness, foster, utilize, elevate, streamline, empower,
comprehensive, realm, flywheel, ecosystem, unpack, navigate, pioneering,
seamless, robust, innovative, cutting-edge, game-changer, revolutionary,
disrupt, transform, synergy, holistic, dynamic, bespoke, curated,
impactful, resonate, propel, catalyst, unprecedented, multifaceted
```

### Why These Words Matter
Rally.fun's AI specifically looks for these words because they are overwhelmingly used by LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) when generating content. Real humans on X/Twitter almost never use words like "delve" or "tapestry" in casual tweets. Using even one of these words can drop your Compliance score to 0.

## Compliance Rules (MANDATORY)

### Must Have
- `@RallyOnChain` mention (naturally integrated, not at the start)
- Clear, human-sounding language
- Proper grammar with natural contractions

### Must NOT Have
- **No hashtags** (#crypto, #web3, etc.) — Rally.fun requires no hashtags
- **No em-dashes** (—, –, --) — use regular dashes or rephrase
- **No starting with @mention** — @RallyOnChain should be in the body, not the beginning
- **No AI banned words** — see full list above
- **No template phrases** — see list above
- **No emojis in excess** — 0-1 emoji max, placed naturally
- **No URL shorteners** — use full URLs or no URL at all

## Scoring Dimensions (Detailed)

### 1. Authenticity (0-3 points)
- **3**: Sounds exactly like a real person's tweet. Natural flow, personal voice, no AI patterns
- **2**: Mostly human-sounding but has slight formal/structured feel
- **1**: Noticeably AI-generated feel; uses some formal language
- **0**: Clearly AI-generated; uses banned words or template phrases

### 2. Relevance (0-3 points)
- **3**: Directly addresses the campaign mission; accurate project representation
- **2**: Related to the campaign but slightly off-topic or generic
- **1**: Loosely related; could apply to any crypto project
- **0**: Not relevant to the campaign at all

### 3. Engagement (0-3 points)
- **3**: Would generate replies, likes, retweets; has a hook or provocative element
- **2**: Decent but doesn't stand out in a timeline
- **1**: Generic; scrolls past without a second look
- **0**: Boring; no reason to interact

### 4. Originality (0-3 points)
- **3**: Unique angle; not something 100 other people would tweet
- **2**: Somewhat unique but follows common patterns
- **1**: Generic take that many others would write
- **0**: Copy-paste feeling; uses template phrases

### 5. Clarity (0-3 points)
- **3**: Crystal clear message; instant understanding
- **2**: Clear but takes a moment to parse
- **1**: Confusing or ambiguous
- **0**: Doesn't make sense or contradicts itself

### 6. Compliance (0-3 points)
- **3**: Perfect compliance; no violations
- **2**: Minor issues (e.g., one borderline word)
- **1**: Multiple violations
- **0**: Major violations (banned words, hashtags, missing @RallyOnChain)

## Tips for High Scores (15+)

1. **Write like you're texting a friend** — casual, direct, no corporate language
2. **Have a real opinion** — even if it's small, opinions score higher than facts
3. **Be specific** — mention a specific feature, event, or detail about the project
4. **Use contractions** — "don't" not "do not", "can't" not "cannot"
5. **Keep it short** — shorter tweets often score higher for clarity and engagement
6. **No filler words** — every word should earn its place
7. **Sound excited but not fake** — genuine enthusiasm, not marketing speak
